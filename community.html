MEET THE COMMUNITY


Community Timeline
1899-1902
Second Anglo Boer War
1901
Comodoro Rivadavia incorporated as a town
April 1902
First group of Boers depart Cape Town for Argentina on the La Pampa from Cape Town
June 1902
First group of Boers arrive at Comodoro Rivadavia (Chubut province, Argentina)
1907
End of migration, with a total of about 600 Afrikaners settled in Comodoro Rivadavia area
December 1907
Discovery of oil
1926
Die Nederduits Gereformeerde Vrouebond (Dutch Reformed Women’s Association) is founded
1938
About half of the Afrikaner community repatriate to South Africa
1953
Last official contact between South Africa and Patagonian Afrikaners
1954
First record of Dutch Reformed Church minutes in Spanish
1960's
Afrikaans is still the most commonly spoken language in Sarmiento (Chubut province, Argentina)
1995
Brian du Toit publishes “Colonia Boer: An Afrikaner Settlement in Chubut, Argentina”
May 2014
UM Fieldwork trip (Andries Coetzee, Lorenzo Garcia-Amaya, Nick Henriksen) to the community
March 2016
Cinema release of “The Boers at the End of the World” in South Africa
