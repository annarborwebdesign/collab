RESEARCH FINDINGS

Spanish By Group

It is clear that Spanish speakers use the same vowel space, whether they are monolingual or bilingual. This suggests that there is not an effect of Afrikaans phonology among bilinguals.

Monolinguals

As expected, monolinguals in each language have fairly different vowel spaces. Afrikaans monolinguals have a particularly high degree of variation in their vowels, as shown by the spread of the ellipses.

BILINGUALS

Bilinguals' vowel space is roughly equivalent regardless of which language is being spoken, and this vowel space resembles that of Spanish monolinguals. This, again, suggests that bilinguals—at least with respect to their vowels—use more Spanish-like phonology in their production of vowels.

Afrikaans By Group

Afrikaans monolinguals have significantly more variation in their vowel production, which may be accounted for with documented allophonic variations. What is interesting, however, is that Afrikaans bilinguals do not use the same degree of variation.
